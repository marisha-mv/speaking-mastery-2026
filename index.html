The 3-day arc — how the message primes the close
Day 1

The awakening

Your voice is your most valuable untapped asset. Show her what's holding her back isn't talent or nerve — it's the absence of an influence system. Reframe the problem so she feels the gap.

Job: problem-awareness · open the loop
Day 2

The taste

Feel what it's like to move a room. Deliver one real framework and a first win — a piece of the One Talk, a persuasion principle she can use today. She thinks: I can actually do this.

Job: build belief · prove it's learnable
Day 3

The decision

From a taste to a skill you own for life. A 3-day taste isn't mastery — and it isn't monetized. Reveal the full path, then make the offer. The close becomes the obvious next step.

Job: reveal the gap · close to the Mastery
The pivot — and the one rule that protects it

By Day 3 she's felt the skill is real and learnable, and that a taste isn't enough — that's what makes the offer land. The rule: the summit must deliver a genuine felt win, and the Mastery must actually rebuild the conversion content (Part 5 was the weakest module last year). Over-promise at the summit and under-deliver in the Mastery, and we burn the warm list the whole launch depends on.
